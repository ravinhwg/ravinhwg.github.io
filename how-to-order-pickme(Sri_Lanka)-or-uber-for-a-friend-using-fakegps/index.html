How to order a PickMe(Sri Lanka) or an Uber for a friend using fake GPS coordinates.

October 12, 2019

My aunt once wanted me to order a cab using PickMe (which is a popular ride hailing app in Sri Lanka). Piece of cake right? but the problem? We were 20km apart. So I tried to change the pickup loaction in PickMe to her place,heck looks like people who made pickme made it very hard to change the pickup location to more than lets say 3km from your actual location. The pin kept coming to my actul location after I tap the drop location.I think thats how the app is designed.To make sure that you enter your actual location to save that poor driver from all that extra driving.

So Lets get started.

1.Go to Google Play Store and search for “Fake GPS”

2.Launch Fake GPS location app

3.Go to settings app and tap on “Mock location app”

4.Select “fake GPS” from the menu

5.Go back to the app select location and press the play button

Now you can trick either PickMe or Uber to use the given mock loaction as the actual location.

6.Pull down to make sure that you are using fake gps to mock location.

Bingo now Uber thinks that you are actually from the location given in the Fake GPS app. Now use your favourite ride hailing app and get going xD
